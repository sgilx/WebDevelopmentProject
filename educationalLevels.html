What Path is Best for You?

First, let's dicsuss the NCLEX

The NCLEX or, National Council Licensure Examination, is an adaptive exam necessary for individuals to pass in order to begin work as an entry level nurse. It determines comptetency levels of aspiring nurses, but also is an evaluation of different institution's nursing programs.



  • Bachelor's of Science in Nursing (BSN)
  • Registered Nurse (RN)
  • Certified Nursing Assistant

BSN is the most known option in Nursing. BSN programs are designed to help individuals attain a bachelor's degree within the healthcare umbrella and become a Registered Nurse(RN). BSN's but also complete the NCLEX to become properly certified. Having a BSN increases your chances of attaining higher pay, more opportunities, and employers prefer those with BSNs but it is not typically required.

RN is a licensed individual who also holds an associate degree. RN's, or Registered Nurses, work under their healthcare team. This includes other physicians such as RT's, Doctors, etc. RN's are those who typically work in schools, doctors offices, clinics, or hospitals. Those pursuing an RN licensure must also pass the NCLEX.

Technically speaking, CNA's are not nurses. They are assistants. Hence their title, Certified Nursing Assistant. However, because they respond to RN's, they are able to gain essential skills and experience. You are able to learn basic healthcare skills and determine hwether you see yourself being able to further your education and pursue to have a BSN or become an RN.
