What if this is only a starting point for me?

Job Outlook

Nursing is an in demand job. It is projected to have a positive outlook within the next 8 years, increasing by 6%. As of May 2022, the Bureau of Labor Statistics declared the median pay for an RN is $81,220 per year. This of course varies depending on location, experience, etc.

Nursing Specialties & Travel Nursing

Different units that people can pursue work in once becoming a nurse vary. Some people will pursue a pediatric specialty, critical care, cardiac, labor & delivery, etc. Each of these specialties will require a residency and training in that particular unit. We urge you to consider this in your decision making as well. When talking Travel Nursing, we are talking about any specialty of nurse, being expected to travel to any destination necessary where their aid is needed. Pay is much greater, but more is expected. One must be prepared to face new environments, teams, etc. The pay difference is as much as $30 per hour.

Connections to Other Medical Occupations

With a background in nursing, a multitude of doors open in your professional career. Nursing is an occupation that will always be in demand, but people may want to pursue other options. Some of these include:

  • Nurse Practictioner
  • CRNA
  • Midwife
  • Nurse Educator
